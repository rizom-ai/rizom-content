---
title: Outcome-Based Cooperation
---
Rizom distinguishes between inputs, outputs, and outcomes in its approach to cooperation. Inputs are resources such as time, money, and materials, while outputs are the deliverables produced through work, including documents, meeting minutes, or software. Outcomes represent the value created by those deliverables rather than the deliverables themselves. Rizom explicitly rejects money as the only measure of value and instead connects value to progress toward its mission. In the current framework, ambassadors who share Rizom’s mission and vision are treated as an important outcome. This approach shifts attention from activity and production toward meaningful effects and community development.
