We are the Rizom Work team: a small consulting and implementation group focused on coordination, transactive memory systems, and human-AI collaboration.

We work with teams that are already capable, but increasingly constrained by invisible knowledge, unclear ownership, brittle handoffs, and decision paths that live in people's heads. AI tends to expose those gaps before it fixes them. Our job is to help teams see the system they are already operating in, then redesign the parts that make good work harder than it needs to be.

The team combines ecosystem architecture, facilitation, operations, communication, TMS research, and AI engineering. That mix matters: coordination problems are rarely just cultural, technical, or managerial. They sit between those categories.

Rizom Work is part of Rizom Collective, but this brain represents the working team behind the Rizom Work practice: the people developing the methodology, running workshops, translating research into operating models, and helping organizations install better knowledge and coordination infrastructure.
