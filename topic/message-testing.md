---
title: Message Testing
---
Message testing is the practice of validating claims, wording, and positioning before communicating them externally. It helps refine drafts by comparing versions, gathering early feedback, and checking whether a message is clear, credible, and resonant with its intended audience. As a validation step rather than a final deliverable, it reduces uncertainty, surfaces the strongest framing, and helps prevent unverified language, weak positioning, or inaccurate promises. It is especially useful when communication quality affects trust, clarity, or conversion, and when there is value in iterating before publishing more broadly.
