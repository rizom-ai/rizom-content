---
title: Knowledge Architecture
---
Knowledge architecture is the practice of structuring information so it can be organized, connected, retrieved, reused, and maintained as a system grows. It treats the arrangement of content as a design problem, not just the content itself, and focuses on creating clear, navigable structures that support long-term retrieval, updates, and expansion. Strong knowledge architecture improves clarity, consistency, and scalability across notes, content systems, and team workflows, helping information remain useful as it accumulates and evolves.
