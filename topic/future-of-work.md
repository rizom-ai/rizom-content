---
title: Future of Work
---
The post argues that the future of work should be organized around play rather than industrial-era routines. Play is defined as voluntary engagement, creative agency, freedom to fail, and the integration of learning with doing. The article presents contemporary work as alienated from purpose, constrained by factory-era assumptions, and shaped by collective patterns that discourage meaningful change. It argues that burnout and talent waste reflect systemic misalignment rather than individual failure. AI is described as a force that can remove routine work and reveal the importance of creativity, connection, and meaning-making. The proposed future centers on work that is aligned with human nature, personal values, and meaningful contribution.
