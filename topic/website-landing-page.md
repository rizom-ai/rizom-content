---
title: Website Landing Page
---
A website landing page is a focused entry point for a site or campaign, designed to communicate an organization’s identity, value proposition, and next step with clarity and persuasion. Strong landing pages combine sharp positioning, clear hierarchy, concise copy, and a compelling call to action to guide visitors toward a specific outcome. They should reflect the broader site structure and messaging system while still staying tightly oriented around conversion and user action. Effective landing page work includes both messaging and page setup: defining the page’s framing, writing the initial content, and establishing a structure that makes the page easy to understand, persuasive, and quick to assemble from a blank start. Landing page conversion should be treated as its own capability, because strong general writing does not automatically produce effective page performance. Improvement often requires direct testing and iteration on homepage or landing page language, positioning, narrative flow, content organization, and page architecture. Reusable templates can help, but the goal remains the same: build trust quickly, present value clearly, and make the desired action easy to take, while recognizing that strong conversion messaging may still require additional design or implementation support and should be validated through testing.
