## What changed since the 2025 edition

The 2025 book described a collaboration platform we hadn't built yet — matching
algorithms, facilitators, Cores, equity protocols — illustrated with fictional
characters (Sarah, Marcus, Amazon's "Tributary") presented as evidence. Since
then the product became real and concrete: **the brain**, an open-source AI
agent grown from your own knowledge, and a network where those agents find each
other by meaning. The brand now leads with what ships.

Three structural changes:

1. **The product is the brain.** Everything starts with one professional's
   knowledge, switched on. Teams and the network grow from there — you → team →
   network, each layer making the others smarter.
2. **One practice, three faces.** Rizom is one thing seen from three sides:
   the tools (open-source platform, `/brain`), the service (workshops and
   consulting, `/work`), and the source (the non-profit foundation that holds
   the IP and stewards the methodology, `/foundation`). One brain, one site.
3. **The expressed metaphor is light.** The rhizome remains our name and our
   root system — the underground network. What people *see* is what grows from
   it: lights in the dark. One light, then constellations, then a living map.

Retired: Sarah/Marcus visiontype narratives, the Amazon "Tributary" story,
invented statistics (48 hours, 73%, 40%), the four protocols, facilitator and
Core terminology. None of these describe the shipped product; none may be used
as evidence. Our proof is now real and verifiable — see Proof Points.

## The Core Problem We Solve

The industrial model of work creates an impossible bind: you're too
specialized for corporate generalism, but too isolated for independent
success. AI sharpened the stakes rather than resolving them — it is not taking
your job; it is exposing how much of your talent you've been wasting.

The bind shows up as three concrete failures — the dark around the lights:

1. **Your best thinking never ships.** Notes, drafts, and ideas scattered
   everywhere. Turning them into something the world can see takes so long
   that most of it dies in your head.
2. **Your team forgets what it knows.** People leave, context disappears, and
   hard-won expertise gets buried in chat threads no one will ever search.
3. **The right people never find each other.** Collaborators matched by job
   titles and résumés, not by what they actually know. The perfect expert
   might be one connection away.

## Our Solution

### It starts with one light — yours

A **brain** turns your notes, essays, and judgment into an agent that answers,
publishes, and represents you — open source, on your own server, in your own
voice. That's the product. Three commands to start.

### Lights find each other

You → team → network. Each layer makes the others smarter:

- **You** — a personal brain. Capture what you know; your brain publishes in
  your voice. *Available now.*
- **Team** — brains connect into shared memory that outlasts any individual.
  *The team bundle.*
- **Network** — distributed expertise; the network matches work to minds by
  substance, not titles. *Emerging.*

We are honest about which layer is which. Never present the emerging layers as
finished.

### One practice, three faces

- **The tools** (`/brain`) — open-source AI agents built from your own
  knowledge. The platform, the code, the self-hosted product.
- **The service** (`/work`) — workshops and consulting that apply the
  methodology in live engagements. One session, one map.
- **The source** (`/foundation`) — the non-profit that holds the IP and
  stewards the methodology independently. Essays and gatherings.

The three faces are one practice. Copy for any face should be legible from the
other two.

## Brand Positioning

### One-Line Positioning

**Your knowledge, switched on — in a network where expertise is alive.**

### Expanded Positioning

Rizom builds the infrastructure for living expertise. Your brain is yours:
open source, running on your own server, speaking in your own voice, owned by
you rather than rented from a platform. Brains connect into team memory and a
network that matches people by what they actually know. We're not building a
walled garden — we're growing a commons, with the methodology held by an
independent foundation.

### What We Are NOT

- **Not a chatbot wrapper.** A brain is grown from one person's real knowledge
  and represents them; it is not a general-purpose assistant with a skin.
- **Not a data play.** Your brain runs on your server. We never hold your
  knowledge hostage; the code is open source.
- **Not a freelance marketplace.** The network matches by meaning and
  substance, not by listing gigs against résumés.
- **Not technology alone.** The tools ship with a practice — workshops,
  methodology, research — and a culture of ownership.
- **Not utopia.** The network layer is emerging, and we say so. We show real
  numbers, even when they're small.

## Brand Voice

### Tone Principles

**Evocative yet concrete.** Every metaphor lands on something you can click,
run, or read. "This site is a brain — talk to it."

**Owner to owner.** We speak to professionals as peers who will own their
tools, not as users to be onboarded into someone else's platform.

**Honest about status.** Available now / the team bundle / emerging. Real
counts on the live map. No invented case studies, no fabricated statistics.

**Warm provocation.** We're allowed to sting — "AI is exposing how much of
your talent you've been wasting" — when the next sentence opens a door.

### Signature Phrases (live on the site)

- "The future of work is play."
- "Your knowledge, switched on."
- "It starts with one light — yours."
- "This is what expertise looks like when it's alive."
- "The dark around the lights."
- "Lights find each other."
- "Matched by substance, not titles."
- "This site is a brain — running the platform it describes."
- "Work becomes play with purpose." *(carried over — still true)*

### Language to Embrace

- Light, lights, switched on, alive, constellation, map, glow
- Brain, agent, voice, publish, represent, capture
- Own, open source, your own server, in your own voice
- Substance, meaning, what you actually know
- Grow, cultivate, emerge, thrive (the rhizome speaks through these)
- Play with purpose

### Language to Avoid

- Disrupt, revolutionize, 10x (we grow, we don't demolish)
- Optimize, leverage, synergize (industrial extraction language)
- Fabricated proof: invented clients, invented percentages, fictional
  competitors — if it didn't happen, it isn't evidence
- "AI-powered" as a selling point (everyone says it; we show it instead)
- Platform-speak that implies we hold the user's data or identity
- Promising the network as if it were finished

## The Metaphor System

### Expressed metaphor: Lights in the dark

This is what visitors see and what the copy speaks:

- **One light** — a single professional's knowledge, switched on
- **The dark around the lights** — what happens to expertise without a network
- **Constellations** — teams; lights that found each other
- **The living map** — the network section: every light is a working agent

The metaphor is functional, not decorative: the proximity map literally renders
agents as lights, placed by semantic distance. Never use the light imagery for
something the product doesn't actually do.

### Root metaphor: The rhizome

The rhizome is our name and our substrate — the underground network the lights
grow from. It explains *how* the network grows: no center, no single point of
failure, connections forming underground before they surface. Use it for
depth (naming, foundation essays, methodology) rather than surface copy.
Growth language — cultivate, emerge, thrive — is the rhizome speaking without
being named.

**The relationship in one line:** the rhizome is the root system; the lights
are what you see at night where it surfaced.

### The One-Metaphor Test

Before publishing, ask: is light the primary image, with growth language in
support? Mixing a third system (machines, factories, sports, music) dilutes
both. One clear sky, one root system.

## Visual Identity

The visual system is the metaphor made literal: **amber lights against a deep
night sky**, with a warm paper daylight mode.

### Color

- **The light (brand/accent):** burnt amber `#b8410c`, glowing to `#ff8b3d`
  in the dark theme. Used the way lights are used — sparingly, warmly, where
  something is alive (CTAs, active agents, accents).
- **The dark (night mode):** deep space ink `#0a0819`, with `#18132a` ink for
  surfaces. This is the sky the lights live in.
- **The paper (light mode):** warm parchment `#f4ede0` / `#faf4e6` — organic,
  not sterile white.
- **The undergrowth (secondary):** deep purple `#4b2a63` and its lighter
  `#8c82c8` — the rhizome tones, supporting rather than leading.

Both themes are first-class; the toggle is part of the brand (day and night
over the same landscape).

### Typography

- **Display: Fraunces** — a warm, characterful serif with optical sizing;
  confident but not corporate. Italics carry the accent phrases
  (*switched on*, *yours*).
- **Body: IBM Plex Sans** — humanist, highly readable, generous spacing.
- **Mono: IBM Plex Mono** — for the working-proof layer: commands, agent
  handles, lexicon names, map coordinates. The mono voice is how the product
  shows through the prose.

### Motifs

- **The proximity map** — the hero visual: agents as lights placed by semantic
  distance. Live data, never a decorative fake.
- **Markers and legends** — `●` professional, `∴` team, `◌` collective,
  `·` archived trace. Cartography, not dashboard.
- **Numbered darkness** — problem statements as `01 / 02 / 03` against the
  dark; light sections answer them.
- **Section caps with asides** — "It starts with one light *— yours*";
  "Lights find each other *— you → team → network*".

### Motion

Lights come on; they don't pop. Staggered reveals, gentle glow, breathing
pulses on live elements. Nothing mechanical, nothing snapped. The map drifts
like a night sky, not like a screensaver.

### Imagery

Prefer the product itself (the live map, real published pages, real terminal
commands) over stock photography. When photography is needed: real working
moments, never staged handshakes.

## Implementation Principles

### Always

- **Show the working proof first** — the site is a brain; the map is live;
  the code is public. Demonstrations beat claims.
- **Lead with light, root with growth** — light is the expressed metaphor;
  rhizome language supports underground.
- **Label the layers honestly** — available now / team bundle / emerging.
- **Keep ownership explicit** — your server, your voice, your agent, open
  source. Every piece of copy should survive the question "who owns this?"
- **Write from the three faces as one practice** — tools, service, source.
- **Ground every metaphor in a feature** — if the light imagery describes
  something the product doesn't do, cut one or build the other.

### Never

- **Invent evidence** — no fictional customers, statistics, or competitors.
  Real numbers only, however small.
- **Promise the emerging layers as finished** — the network earns its copy as
  it grows.
- **Use extraction language** — optimize, leverage, synergize.
- **Hide the smallness** — two agents on the map is a beginning, not an
  embarrassment. Honesty is the differentiator AI marketing can't copy.
- **Let the brand float free of the product** — every brand claim should be
  one click from its proof.

## Success Metrics for Brand Health

**Clarity:**

- A visitor can say what a brain is in one sentence: "an open-source agent
  built from my own knowledge that answers and publishes as me."
- The three faces are legible: tools / service / source.
- "Lights" is understood as the network of working agents, not decoration.

**Trust:**

- Live, small numbers increase credibility rather than undermining it.
- "Open source, your own server" is recalled as the ownership story.
- The talk-to-the-site moment converts skeptics: the proof is interactive.

**Resonance:**

- "Your best thinking never ships" lands viscerally with the target audience.
- "The future of work is play" is associated with Rizom and the founding
  essay.
- The night-sky visual system is recognized across the site, the map, and
  materials.
