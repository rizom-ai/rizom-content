---
title: Messaging Validation
---
The content highlights that first-draft messaging should not be treated as final until it has been tested. It points to a need to check whether the intended message actually lands with an audience before relying on it as the basis for further work. This suggests an iterative approach where positioning is created, then validated through feedback or observation. The topic matters because weak or untested messaging can undermine the effectiveness of a page or initiative. It also implies that clarity and resonance are separate from simply having a draft. In practice, the topic is about confirming that communication works outside the creator's own perspective.
