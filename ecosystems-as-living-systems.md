Yeehaa.io says ecosystems are best understood as living systems organized through **trust, memory, governance, coordination, and shared values**—“trust, memory, and governance made visible.”

It describes **ecosystem architecture** as designing and cultivating organizations and communities without forcing them into rigid hierarchies or fixed blueprints. The practice has four movements:

1. **Align** around shared values and a moral challenge  
2. **Validate** active commitment and contribution  
3. **Scale** distributed responsibility  
4. **Spin out** toward self-governance  

The central principle is: ecosystems should be **designed and cultivated, but not forced**—more like farming than conventional business building.
