---
title: Message Testing
---
Message testing is the practice of validating claims, wording, and positioning before using them externally. It helps refine drafts by comparing versions, gathering early feedback, and checking whether the message is clear, credible, and resonant with its intended audience. This makes it a practical safeguard against unverified language, weak positioning, or inaccurate promises, especially when communication quality affects trust, clarity, or conversion. The process reduces risk and improves the likelihood that outward-facing messaging will land well across different contexts.
